Why You Need an AI Agent

Not a pitch about the future — a plain look at the week you’re already having.

Where Your Week Actually Goes

Scheduling and rescheduling. Catching up on meetings you missed. Remembering who’s waiting on what. None of it is hard — it’s just constant, and it adds up to hours.

A part-time assistant can run thousands a year — and clocks out at five. Your agent is there from six in the morning to eleven at night, as long as your computer’s on.

What It Takes Off Your Plate

Your Calendar, Handled

Ask what’s on, have it move things, let it book the follow-up — by messaging it like you’d text a person.

Your Meetings, Remembered

With the Meeting Notes add-on, it syncs your notes and answers questions like “what did we decide on Friday?”

Your Mornings, Briefed

A routine can message you a briefing before you’re up — today’s calendar, what needs attention — without being asked.

An Agent Is Not a Chatbot

A chatbot

Answers when you sit down to ask. When you close the tab, it’s done — the work is still yours to do.

Your agent

Runs on a schedule, remembers what you tell it, and messages you first when it finishes something — in the chat apps you already use.

And What It Can’t Do

Archie can’t spend your money — that ability doesn’t exist in the app. It speaks only in the chats you connect it to, to people you’ve approved. And your conversations and files live on your own computer, not on our servers. The full, honest list — including what we’re still building — is on our Trust page.
